Effects

An effect is an audio processor. It accepts audio input and produces an audio output that is a modification of the input. Common effects include echos, reverb, and others. The effects component will accept audio from the other components and apply the effects, producing the final output of the system. The number of effects features you support will determine your grade. The following are features of an effects system:

Controllable effects sends

An effects box can be considered to be a single input device that produces an output. We call the input the "dry" signal. The output is then "wet". Controllable effects sends means you can control the mixture of wet and dry for every effect and every audio source. So, no effects would be applied to one sound, while three are applied to another. Generally, a professional mixing system will have a certain number of "effects sends". that send sound to an effect. Then the output of all effects can be mixed. Your effects may be in series or parallel. Series effects are considered to be only a single effects send.
